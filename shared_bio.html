Aiwei Zhang

I am a senior undergraduate student at Dartmouth College majoring in Computer Science.

I currently conduct research at the Dartmouth AI and Mental Health: Innovation in Technology-Guided Healthcare (AIM HIGH) Laboratory , advised by Professor Nicholas Jacobson, where I focus on developing machine learning models and techniques for mental health applications. My recent work, in collaboration with Professor SouYoung Jin, explores wearable actigraphy data and foundation models for behavioral insights.

I have also conducted research at the Dartmouth Reality and Robotics Lab (RLab) advised by Professor Alberto Quattrini Li, where I contributed to advancing autonomous navigation for marine robotics and collaborating on a project for a multi-modal perception dataset for autonomous surface vehicles.

At Dartmouth, my research has been generously supported by multiple fundings, including the Dartmouth Neukom Institute for Computational Science and the James O. Freedman Presidential Scholarship. I have also completed a rigorous curriculum, including seven graduate-level CS courses, and earned five academic citations for excellence in CS coursework.

Outside of academics, I enjoy mountain biking, skiing, and scuba diving, and I have been playing the cello for 13 years.
