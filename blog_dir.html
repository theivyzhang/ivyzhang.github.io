[COMING SOON] Commentary: Silent Contributions: Unveiling the Social Class Disparities Behind NLP's Invisible Workforce

03-01-2024

This is a final paper I wrote for my class - COSC72 Computational Linguistics, where we were prompted to think about the social impacts of AI and NLP. Being a student researcher at Dartmouth has exposed me to various opporunities (e.g. projects and funding) aimed at furthering my academic and career journies. When I researched about an often overlooked problem of AI - the widening inequalities within the technology industry's workforce, I am reminded of how lucky I am to be in a supportive environment like Dartmouth, and how I should position myself as a responsible and thoughtful member of this industry, and recognize the people who have worked many hours to produce the datasets and information I am currently using in my research.

What I learnt from 72 hours of scuba diving

06-21-2023 |[read me]

My first step to a marine roboticist career is sitting down and writing my first piece of ROS code. However, my first daunting commitment to this career is getting scuba diving certified: overcoming my fear of the water and sticking with my promise. My 72 hours of intense diving was accompanied by the signature unforgiving rainstorms of Hong Kong. On top of the turbulences and murky ocean water, it has taught me to respect and love the ocean we're surrounded by and take a moment to immerse ourselves in.
