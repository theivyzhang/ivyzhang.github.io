Lesson #1: If you made a commitment, be true to your word.

My three days of in-person diving sessions were all accompanied by rainstorms and even occasional lightning.

As rain poured relentlessly from the sky, obscuring visibility and creating an atmosphere of uncertainty, I found myself facing the daunting task of completing 24 diving tasks. With each task, my wetsuit became heavier and more saturated, clinging to my body like a second skin. Yet, amidst the discomfort and the temptation to give in to the elements, I forged ahead, determined to honor the commitment I had made to myself, my coach, my research advisor and colleagues.

My coach later told me that most people who gave up scuba diving gave up on just the 3rd task - underwater mask retrieval.

True character is revealed not in the absence of obstacles, but in the unwavering dedication to overcome them.

In those moments, I discovered the power of resilience and the satisfaction that comes from pushing beyond self-imposed limitations. Scuba diving taught me that when we make a commitment, it is our duty to honor it, regardless of the obstacles that may come our way. It is in keeping our promises, even when the path seems arduous and uncomfortable, that we build trust, strength, and ultimately, a sense of self-worth.

I had 24 chances to bail out. I had 24 milestones to prove my resilience.
